From Pens to Keyboards: How Technology Changed My Way of Learning

I never thought learning would move so fast from notebooks to screens. When the pandemic hit during my Grade 10 year, I was suddenly pushed into online classes that I wasnâ€™t ready for at all. New tools, unstable internet, and constant pressure became part of my daily routine. Looking back now, I realize that this sudden shift completely changed how I learn and how I see technology. At first, I felt lost because everything I was used to suddenly disappeared. Traditional classrooms were replaced by virtual meetings that felt unfamiliar and confusing. This experience marked the beginning of my journey toward adapting to digital learning.

Adjusting to Online Learning

Challenges and Discoveries

When classes shifted online, everything felt overwhelming and unfamiliar. I did not even own a laptop at first because I never thought I would need one for school. We had to buy a laptop just so I could attend online classes and submit requirements. This experience made me realize how dependent education has become on technology, even for students who were not prepared for it. One of my biggest struggles was dealing with slow internet and sudden disconnections. There were times when I missed discussions or got disconnected during quizzes, which made me anxious and frustrated. I also had to learn how to use LMS and Google Classroom on my own. Through this, I learned that technology is only helpful when access and connectivity are reliable.

Growing Confidence

Embracing New Skills

Online learning did not only affect me academically but also emotionally. I felt anxious every time I had to recite because opening the microphone and camera felt intimidating. Unlike face-to-face classes, I became more conscious knowing that everyone was listening and watching. Over time, I realized that this anxiety pushed me to slowly build confidence in expressing myself.

Mastering Tools

Becoming Tech-Savvy

As time passed, technology started to feel less intimidating and more helpful. Canva played a big role in developing my creativity and designing skills, especially when making school presentations and projects. Using Google Workspace also helped me stay organized with documents, files, and deadlines. These tools showed me that technology can enhance learning when used properly and creatively.

Looking Back

Reflections as an IT Student

Now that I am an IT student at UE, I realize how valuable those early experiences were. The adjustment I went through during the pandemic helped me adapt more easily to technology in my current field. From pens to keyboards, I learned how to fully embrace digital tools that are essential in IT. This made me more confident that I chose the right path for my future career. I also became more independent in solving technical problems on my own. Each challenge helped me improve my patience and problem-solving skills. These experiences shaped my mindset toward continuous learning and improvement.
